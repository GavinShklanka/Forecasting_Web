Welcome to the Forecasting Experiment

In this experiment, you’ll act as a decision-maker forecasting future demand in several different markets. Each market behaves differently — some are calm and predictable, while others are noisy or changing rapidly.

Noise vs. Change

Noise = random short-term fluctuations that don’t reflect a real shift.

Example: A one-day weather blip spikes sales, but the overall trend is unchanged.

Change = a genuine, lasting shift in average behavior (growth/decline).

Example: Sales steadily rise for months as the area gains new residents.

Your Goal

You will complete six short conditions, each with different levels of noise and change. In each round, you’ll see recent data and enter your next forecast. React to meaningful shifts, but avoid overreacting to random noise.
